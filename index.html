gradeSenior Project

I collaborated with 5 other students to create KittyPath, a Google Chrome extension meant to aggregate all of Penn State's scheduling info into one place. We observed that the current scheduling site, LionPath, is slow and the user must look in many different places to get the information they need. With KittyPath, the user installs the extension, navigates to their LionPath Academic requirements once, and the extension pulls all of the relevant information using a content script. The user can then view their requirements to graduate, look up courses offered next semester, and even get recommendations based on what is available and their recommended academic plan. Users can save courses for later, and have the extension directly inject the courses to their LionPath schedule Builder. Won first place for Computer Science during the Penn State Harrisburg Capstone Design Conference 2018.
